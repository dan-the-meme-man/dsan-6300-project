4-Day Moving Average of Number of Flights (March '22)

This line chart displays the 4-day moving average of the number of flights in March 2022. The moving average is calculated by taking the average of the number of flights on a given day and the three days preceding it.

The x-axis represents the day of the month (i.e. Day 20 = March 20, 2022), while the y-axis represents the 4-day moving average of the number of flights. For instance, the 4-day moving average of the number of flights on March 20, 2022 was about 18,701 flights, indicating that the average number of flights per day over the period March 17-20, 2022 was about 18,701.

There is a weak cyclic trend in the data, with a spike roughly once a week (ignoring the spike around day 4). The spikes occur around Sundays and Mondays (6-7, 13-14, 20-21, and 27-28), which may be due to increased daily flights on weekends. Though this conflicts with the earlier observation that Tuesdays-Thursdays had the most flights in March 2022, it is still possible because again, there were more of those days in March 2022 than there were Mondays, Fridays, Saturdays, or Sundays.
